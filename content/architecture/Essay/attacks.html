Why Attack the World Trade Center?

Though the architect of the original World Trade Center hoped the twin towers would become a symbol of power and democracy for Americans, he could not have imagined what they would represent for the terrorists who destroyed the structures. For them, the World Trade Center represented the control Americans held over the world economy and culture (Protetch, ix). This view must have only been heightened by the prominence of the Towers in New York's skyline. As two of the tallest structures in New York, they were in more danger of being attacked than any of the surrounding structures.

The 1993 Bombings

The events that took place on September 11 were not the first terrorist attacks on the structure. Another attempt to destroy the tower took place on February 26, 1993, when a car bomb was set off in the parking garage under the World Trade Center. The blast filled both Towers with smoke and killed six people and injured another thousand. As for the damages sustained by the structure, the bomb created an enormous crater in the garage and damaged the floors above it. Despite the damages sustained to the base of the buildings, the Towers did not collapse, which demonstrates the strength and resilience of the structures.

The 2001 Terrorist Attacks

After the attacks on September 11 took place, debates ensued concerning the structural implications of the collapse of the towers. The first hijacked aircraft hit the North Tower at 8:46 and shortly after at 9:03 a second aircraft crashed into the South Tower. The towers did not immediately collapse after impact, demonstrating the efficiency of the structure which was (hypothetically) designed to withstand the impact of a large commercial plane (Bazant 603). After almost an hour of resisting the fire caused by the jet fuel, the towers collapse, damaging some of the surrounding structures as they did. This delay between the planes' impact and the collapse of the towers is the source of the structural discussions regarding the structure. If the planes didn't cause the Twin Towers to immediately collapse, then what ultimately lead to their failure?
