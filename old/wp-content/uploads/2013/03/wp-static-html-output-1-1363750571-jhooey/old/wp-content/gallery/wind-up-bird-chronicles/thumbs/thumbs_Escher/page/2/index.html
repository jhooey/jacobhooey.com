Dec 29, 2011 Project, Software

I finally finished my first video game! Sure it’s only a simple card game, and yes I know it needs some graphical improvements, but HERE IT IS. ASP, LDAP Queries

Sep 16, 2011 Skills

Recently, at work, I have been tasked to update our intranet telephone list from an SQL query to an LDAP query systems to reduce the amount of data entry needed when creating users. The challenge for me was more than a simple code and fix. The reason: I didn’t know ASP, LDAP Querying and my SQL was very basic. Regardless I grab the bull by the horns and jumped in.

During this project I learned ASP, how to query Active Directory using LDAP Queries and expanded my knowledge of both SQL and HTML.

Another thing I learned, on a more personal level, is that I love to code. Taking on a challenge and writing a solution, running into problems and going line by line to find the problem. This type of work really gets my brain working and uses it solve any problem that gets in its way. Therefore I am going to make a greater effort from now on to expand my knowledge of languages, to start my own projects and to direct my career towards a programming position.

Bishop’s University

Aug 30, 2011 Accomplishments, School

Having been outside school and in the real world now for a couple of years now I have had some time to reflect on my past experiences. Going to Bishop’s University was one of those major life decisions that you either look back with fond memories or you might never want to look back at all. Luckily for me it was one of the best decisions I have ever made. Every single moment at Bishops was memorable and all those moments were enjoyable.

What drew me to Bishop’s University in the first place was its size. With an average student population of 2000 I felt it could be a great learning environment, this turned out to be truer than expected. The biggest class I ever attended contained 80 people, but even then by the end of the semester the teacher knew all our names. I found with such small classes (some with only 4 people) it was much easier to ask questions and approach the teacher for advice. Also Teachers were able to tailor their lesson plans to the needs of the students. All in all I found my education to be a great experience, one that I couldn’t get in a class room of 1000

Another thing about unique to Bishop’s University where the unique opportunities to join the world of academia professionally. The first of these opportunities was a position as Teacher’s Assistant for two of my professors. I would correct assignments and would assist in managing the students assignments as a whole. The second, and even greater opportunity, was an USRA scholarship that allowed me to work during the summer as a Research Assistant for Dr. Lorne Nelson. I did work helping a masters student write his thesis, helped build a website for a division of CHEAR (Canadian High-Energy Astrophysics Research). This was an invaluable experience that provided me great insight into the world of academia.

Overall Bishop’s University was an experience that was critical in creating the man I am today. For anyone looking at different universities I give Bishop’s University a very strong recommendation.

McGill University

Jan 15, 2011 Accomplishments, Artwork, School, Skills

Although my time at McGill University was brief, it was an amazing experience and helped me expand my creativity. I learned many things in such a short time it will be hard to describe them all. The main areas of study in the architecture program were Drawing, understanding space, a bit of history and structural engineering.

Drawing was a huge part of the program, every course I took had some drawing component to it. The two main courses that related primarily to the artistic side of architecture were Freehand Drawing and Architecture Studio. Freehand drawing was primarily focused on the artistic side of drawing while the Studio class focused more on drawing in relation to architecture. In both I developed a good knowledge of space and compositions as well as a better understanding of using an artistic medium to convey an idea.

When I said I learned a bit of history that was somewhat of an understatement. The History course offered at McGill was a dense analysis of the overall history of architecture, from the structures of ancient Egypt and Greece to the more modern La Galerie des Machines in paris 1889. I also took a course on structures, which was essentially a history class but took the approach of analyzing the building methods and techniques used throughout history and how they relate to the buildings of today.

Although I only spent a semester at McGill University I felt like I have received a rich education that will last for the rest of my life.
