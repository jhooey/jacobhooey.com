Essay – World Trade Center

Nov 26, 2009 Project

This essay was a collaborative research paper regarding the history, collapse and future of the World Trade Center. This Essay looks at the structural significance of the two towers and how the construction methods contributed to their eventual collapse. We decided to present our essay digitally through the structure of a website. In this way we were able to isolate the important subjects and present them separately, while keeping the overall structure of the essay intact. Also this format provided us with the unique opportunity to incorporate links to videos, which would normally be omitted from a standard pen and paper essay.

If you wish to see the final product feel free to follow this link:

The World Trade Center

Although this was a collaboration the task of programming the website was left completely up to me. We all decided on a free css layout and I modified the structure and code to suit our needs. Although this was not my first website, it definitely was a very good learning experience and I used it to expand my knowledge of css even further.

Birdcage

Oct 23, 2009 Art Gallery, Artwork

The birdcage represents the internal structure of a merger between three birds. The use of rib cages represents the idea of a cage found within a bird that anchors it in its physical form. This is the 3rd project in a series of 4 which all began with bird drawings. The birdcage is bent steel wire soldered together to create rib cage like structures.

Wind-up Bird Chronicle

Oct 4, 2009 Art Gallery, Artwork

Just to state outright that I have never read “Wind-up Bird Chronicle” and all the following pieces of art were created on the impressions I received from a single passage extracted from this book. I just felt like a disclaimer was warranted, if it isn’t it was still fun to write.

Anyway, the below drawings are creations that spawned from reading a short exert of the Wind-up Bird Chronicle. They were all created in a different style, abstract, Escher inspired and clean perspective. Each was a different challenge and I am proud of how all three came out. Even though the perspective is out of place and removed from context (ie. it is a third person perspective derived from a first person narrative).

What really came out from this project for me was a better understanding of space, and how it can be used in a two dimensional context, and that it can be even more interesting when these 2D images are transformed into a three dimensional creation.

Sketches from Freehand Drawing

Sep 30, 2009 Art Gallery, Artwork

These sketches are a clear representation of my artist ability when I started my studies at McGill University. I had to take baby steps, and slowly find my own technique and style. I like these drawings because they are raw and true to myself, they haven’t been worked and reworked, some of them were even completed in 30 seconds, all of them under 5 minutes. Not enough time to think, just to react. Using such a quick paced method really gets you in the mood to draw and I know that I wouldn’t of had the confidence to draw full portraits if I hadn’t started small.

Bird Drawings

Sep 18, 2009 Art Gallery, Artwork

When I began my adventure in architecture I had very little knowledge in the workings of the artistic world. So when the professor detailed the first task of the semester I was both excited and extremely lost. My studio class was asked to draw the movement of a bird. As a recent physics graduate I had no idea how to even begin an art assignment. So I began were most who are based in science begin, with a literal representation of a bird. As you can see below I drew my bird in three stop motion frames of his flight. I know the creativity is lacking but this was a personal test of my artistic ability. I put my effort into creating a the best bird I could muster.

The bird with 3 heads is yet another part of this story. My studio class was asked to take those three drawings and merge them. I chose to superimpose my drawings keeping the body as the central fixed point. The result was a weird and interesting looking creature with 3 heads and 6 wings flying through the air. Looking back I am quite proud of these first drawings and I feel like they were the first stepping stones in my artist journey.

Some technical info for those who are interested; The 3 birds were drawn with pencil on normal drawing paper and the bird composition was done with ink pen on mylar paper.

Final Thesis Presentation

May 21, 2009 Thesis

On the Nature of Dynamical Instabilities

Physics Department
Bishop’s University

Thursday, May 21, 2009
1:00 pm

This presentation will explore the theory behind Binary Star Systems (Cataclysmic Variables) and the assumptions under which we are studying these systems. Also it will reveal the results from my calculations obtained using the methods described below.

Through numerical simulations based on stellar theory and using supercomputers, we are able to theorize about the way binary star systems interact and evolve. For certain initial conditions, the resulting evolutionary tracks are stable. However, given an alternate set of initial conditions, the resulting evolutionary tracks may be highly erratic and unstable.
